  * ## Overview
In addition to her clinical interests in young breast cancer patients, Dr. Lee is active in clinical research, clinical trials, and translational breast cancer research. She is actively involved with her colleagues in investigating and improving fertility preservation education and collaborating with the faculty in Molecular and Functional Imaging in the development of novel agents for intraoperative identification of breast cancer.

  * ## Research Interest
Dr. Lee has been a clinical and translational investigator since her initial appointment at Moffitt. She has been a clinical collaborator in behavioral and outcomes studies as well as translational and biomarker driven work with a particular focus on intraoperative imaging of breast cancer as well as predictors of metastasis. She has written and conducted clinical trials at all phases, including IITs and both pharmacologic and drug trials. She has an open tissue collection protocol for breast cancer patients. In addition to internal and private funding, she has been a recipient of NIH and DOD grants, and continues to actively seek external funding for clinical and translational projects.
